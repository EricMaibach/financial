What are credit spreads?

Credit spreads measure the extra yield investors demand to hold corporate bonds over risk-free Treasuries. Wider spreads = more fear of defaults. Tighter spreads = complacency or genuine credit health.

  • HY OAS (High-Yield Option-Adjusted Spread) — Benchmark for junk bond stress. Normal range: 300–500 bp. Crisis levels: 700+ bp.
  • IG OAS (Investment-Grade OAS) — Blue-chip corporate stress. Normal: 80–150 bp. Stress: 200+ bp.
  • CCC Spread — The distressed-credit leading indicator. When CCC spreads spike before HY, it often signals contagion risk.
  • HY–IG Differential — When this gap widens, HY is underperforming IG — an early risk-off signal.
Watch for these signals
  • HY OAS above 500 bp — Markets pricing in meaningful default risk; typically precedes equity drawdowns
  • CCC leading HY wider — Contagion spreading from distressed to broader credit
  • HY-IG differential widening — Risk-off shift; avoid lower-quality credit
  • Spreads at 10th percentile or below — Historically tight; credit cycle may be late-stage
  • HY ≤ 300 bp — Historically tight; peak credit cycle complacency
  • HY 300–500 bp — Normal range; selective credit opportunities
  • HY 500–700 bp — Stress building; reduce lower-quality exposure
  • HY ≥ 700 bp — Distress / crisis; defaults rising
  • IG ≥ 200 bp — Significant credit stress; investment-grade bonds under pressure
